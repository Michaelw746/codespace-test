The Problem

The unployment rate is 3.7% as of Nov 2022, and the black and hispanic community are dispotionally affected, with an unemployment rate of 8.6%.
The latest economics down trend and inflation escalated this issue and affect black and hispanic communities harder than others.
Watts is a neighborhood that has a population of mostly black and Hispanics family. Hispanics makes up about 74 percent of the watts community and African American makes up about 22 percent of watts community.

"I have time and energy but I don't know where to find a job." - A typical jobless person in a community said.
"I need someone help with the posters but I don't know where to find them." - A coomunity officer said so, looking for some labor.
That is the real problem - information gap in community

The Solution: JobWarrior

JobWarrior's mission is to reduce economic inequality among the black and Hispanic communities by providing the employers with a market place for talent in the Watt's community.

We offer an equal market place that's localized to each community, where employer can post their job supplies and talents can list their demands.
When supply and demand meet, when talent and good will meet, that's where magic going to happen.
